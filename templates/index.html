Social Stories in Medicine

Helping autistic patients feel comfortable in a medical environment.

Intro

Social stories were first invented in 1991 by Carol Gray as a mechanism through which people can prepare for unknown situations. While widely used across various industries, we have found them to be incredibly effective in the medical context with patients with autism. After preparing for appointments by reading through social stories with their caretakers, kids with Autism are able to function very well in an environment that often induces stress and anxiety.
